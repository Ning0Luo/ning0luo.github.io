Research

Ning Luo is a postdoctoral fellow in the computer science department at Northwestern University, working with Xiao Wang. She earned her Ph.D. from Yale University in June 2023, advised by Ruzica Piskac. Ning's research combines formal methods and cryptography to achieve reliability, verifiability, and confidentiality in practical and challenging scenarios. Her work primarily revolves around two main objectives. Firstly, she aims to prove the correctness and safety of all programs, irrespectively of their source code availability. This involves developing privacy-preserving formal verification toolchains that allow program verification without disclosing the program details. Secondly, she works on creating a programming framework to enable developers at all skill levels to implement cryptography-based application which involves combining techniques from program analysis, program synthesis, programming languages, and cryptography.
